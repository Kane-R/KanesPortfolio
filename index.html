so what's so special?

This website is aliive! ..in that it can sense the screen (and even the device) you're viewing it from and adapt acordingly. That's special.

In nerd-talk, this website is responsive, meaning the rows and columns that make up the pages will automatically begin re-aranging and dynamically stacking when the screen size sensed hits thresholds of 1200px, 992px, 768px, and 576px for standard mobile.

Resize the browser window to see the effect. Responsive web design has become more important as the amount of mobile traffic now accounts for the majority of total internet traffic.

my philosophy

I always keep in mind that the end user is a person looking at and operating a screen and nowadays that screen is likely to be on a mobile phone but it could also be any size screen or device ranging from mobile to tablet to desktop to 75inch loungeroom TV to 10m wide outdoor digital display.

I also keep in mind that people's attention is a valuable and hard-faught comodity so content needs to not only be easilly accessable, but it needs to capture and maintain their attention. Content needs to be compelling, easy to consume and as entertaining as possible while staying on point like a laser.
